Acute ischemic strokes
Patient presented with an episode of unresponsiveness followed by clear relatively diffuse left hemispheric dysfunction.  Patient was found to have extensive bilateral acute ischemic strokes thought to be due to hypercoagulability of malignancy despite therapeutic anticoagulation on Xarelto.  CT angiogram shows occlusion of the left intracranial ICA with good filling of the left MCA and ACA.

My primary clinical concern is that patient has a high risk of recurrent ischemic strokes especially given that she experienced a multifocal bilateral ischemic stroke while therapeutically anticoagulated on rivaroxaban.  Although there is theoretically risk of hemorrhagic transformation with therapeutic anticoagulation, a recent study published in the Lancet 2025 (CATALYST) supports that early initiation of therapeutic anticoagulation after ischemic stroke is likely favorable with significantly decreased ischemic events and no significant increase in symptomatic hemorrhage.

Acute encephalopathy
