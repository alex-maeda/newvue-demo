
Impression
Angiography of the right lower extremity demonstrates severe multilevel peripheral serial disease. Multifocal stenoses in the proximal and mid right superficial femoral artery were treated with 6 mm drug coated balloon with good result. Severe trifurcation and distal disease. Multifocal moderate to severe stenoses in the distal third of the anterior tibial artery were treated with 2 mm PTA with some improvement. Dorsalis pedis artery occludes shortly after takeoff and gives rise to small, thready collaterals. Peroneal and posterior tibial arteries occlude several centimeters above the ankle also giving rise to small thready collaterals. No significant reconstitution of the posterior tibial artery and the plantar arch and digital branches are not well seen.
 
  
 Narrative
LOWER EXTREMITY ANGIOGRAPHY AND INTERVENTION
 
** HISTORY **:
85 years old Female, with severe right lower extremity peripheral arterial disease associated with nonhealing wounds on her toes.
 
 
** TECHNIQUE **:
 
PROCEDURE SUMMARY:
- Arterial access with ultrasound guidance
- Right lower extremity angiography as described below
- Arterial interventions as described below
- Post intervention right lower extemity angiography
 
Sedation: Moderate sedation was achieved with intravenous medications including versed 2 mg, benadryl 50 mg and fentanyl 100 mcg.
 
Other intraprocedural medications: Heparin 4000 units intravenous; hydralazine 10 mg intravenous
 
Radiation Dose - Fluoroscopy time (min): 20
 
Contrast: 118 mL of Visipaque 320
 
 
** FINDINGS **:
 
PROCEDURE DETAILS:
 
Informed consent for the procedure including risks, benefits and alternatives was obtained and time-out was performed prior to the procedure.  The access site was prepared and draped using maximal sterile barrier technique including cutaneous antisepsis.
 
Local analgesia was achieved with 2% lidocaine administered subcutaneously. The left common femoral artery was accessed with Micropuncture set with 21 gauge needle and a 5 French vascular sheath was placed.  Left common femoral arteriogram was performed and arteriotomy confirmed to lie in the left common femoral artery.    Omniflush catheter was advanced into the distal abdominal aorta and pelvic angiogram was performed. 
 
Over angled glide wire, omniflush catheter was advanced into the right external iliac artery.  Right lower extremity runoff was performed to the ankle. 
 
Luminal irregularity associated with multifocal moderate to severe stenoses were seen in the proximal/mid right superficial femoral artery. Distal third of the right superficial femoral artery and the popliteal artery demonstrate mild luminal irregularity but no significant stenosis. Anterior tibial artery is patent but demonstrates multifocal moderate to severe stenoses in its distal third. The dorsalis pedis artery is severely irregular and occludes shortly after takeoff giving rise to small, thready collaterals. Tibioperoneal trunk is patent. Posterior tibial artery is diffusely irregular with multifocal moderate to severe stenoses. The posterior tibial artery occludes in its distal third giving rise to tortuous collaterals. No significant reconstitution. Peroneal artery is diffusely irregular with multifocal moderate to severe stenoses. The peroneal artery occludes giving rise to small, tortuous collaterals in its distal third.. Dorsalis pedis artery occludes shortly after takeoff giving rise to small, thready collaterals. The plantar arch is never well seen. Very poor runoff below the ankle.
 
Over 035 advantage wire, the Omni Flush catheter and 5 French vascular sheath were removed and a 6 French by 45 cm destination sheath was placed. The destination sheath tip was positioned in the right common femoral artery. Through manipulation of 035 crossing catheter and the 035 advantage wire, wire/catheter access was established the level of the right popliteal artery. Position confirmed with small contrast injection. The affected right superficial femoral artery was predilated with 5 mm PTA and then the proximal and middle thirds of the right superficial femoral artery were treated with 6 mm DCB. Post intervention there is some residual luminal irregularity but no significant residual stenosis.
 
Through manipulation of 014 advantage wire and 014 crossing catheter, wire access was established to the level of the ankle but the crossing catheter would not pass beyond the middle third of the right anterior tibial artery. Crossing catheter was removed and the affected anterior tibial artery was treated with shockwave javelin device. Javelin device successfully pass to the level of the distal third of the anterior tibial artery. Javelin device was then exchanged for a 2 x 40 mm coyote balloon catheter which was successfully advanced to the level of the ankle joint. The distal third of the anterior tibial artery was treated with 2 mm PTA with some improvement.
 
Vascular sheath was removed and hemostasis achieved with Perclose (suture broke) and manual compression.
 
Complications: No immediate complications.
