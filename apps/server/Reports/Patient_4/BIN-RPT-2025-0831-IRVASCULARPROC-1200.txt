    LOWER EXTREMITY ANGIOGRAPHY AND INTERVENTION
** HISTORY **:    85 years old Female, with progressive tissue loss at the right toes despite his angiography with intervention one month prior. Repeat right lower extremity angiography with intervention requested.
** TECHNIQUE **:       PROCEDURE SUMMARY:  - Arterial access with ultrasound guidance  - Right lower extremity angiography as described below  - Arterial interventions as described below  - Post intervention right lower extemity angiography     Sedation: Moderate sedation was achieved with intravenous medications including versed 2 mg and fentanyl 200 mcg.     Other intraprocedural medications: Heparin 6000 units intravenous; nitroglycerin 400 mcg intra-arterial; alteplase 2 mg intra-arterial   Radiation Dose - Fluoroscopy time (min): 20   Contrast: 105 mL of Visipaque 320
** FINDINGS **:     PROCEDURE DETAILS:     Informed consent for the procedure including risks, benefits and alternatives was obtained and time-out was performed prior to the procedure.  The access site was prepared and draped using maximal sterile barrier technique including cutaneous antisepsis.     Local analgesia was achieved with 2% lidocaine administered subcutaneously. The left common femoral artery was accessed with Micropuncture set with 21 gauge needle and a 5 French vascular sheath was placed.  Left common femoral arteriogram was performed and arteriotomy confirmed to lie in the left common femoral artery.    Omniflush catheter was advanced into the distal abdominal aorta.     Over angled glide wire, omniflush catheter was advanced into the right external iliac artery. 035 advantage wire was advanced into the right superficial femoral artery. The Omni Flush catheter and 5 French vascular sheath were removed and a 6 French by 45 cm destination sheath was placed. The destination sheath tip was positioned in the distal right external iliac artery. Right lower extremity runoff was performed to the foot.   Diffusely slow flow of contrast may be related to decreased cardiac output. Clinical correlation is recommended. Common femoral arteries widely patent. Profunda femoris demonstrates diffuse luminal irregularity with multifocal stenoses. Superficial femoral artery demonstrates diffuse luminal irregularity with multifocal mild narrowing. Popliteal artery is patent. Anterior tibial artery is patent with diffuse luminal irregularity associated with multifocal mild narrowing in its proximal third. Multifocal moderate to severe stenoses are seen in the mid/distal thirds of the anterior tibial artery. The anterior tibial artery crosses the ankle and supplies a patent but diminutive and irregular dorsalis pedis artery. Good the tibioperoneal trunk is patent. The peroneal artery is patent but somewhat diminutive and irregular with multifocal stenoses. The peroneal artery occludes in its distal third several centimeters above the ankle. The posterior tibial artery. It is patent but irregular with multifocal moderate to severe stenoses is seen in its proximal and middle thirds. Directed posterior tibial artery occludes near the junction between the mid and distal thirds. Giving rise to tortuous collaterals. The plantar arch is not well seen. Few, spidery digital branches are seen coursing toward the toes.     Technique utilizing an 014 angled crossing catheter and 014 advantage wire, this the right anterior tibial artery was selectively catheterized. Wire/catheter access established in the distal third of the anterior tibial artery. Position confirmed with small contrast injection. Multifocal stenoses in AT treated with 2 mm PTA from distal AT into proximal DP and 2.5 mm PTA from the origin of the AT into its distal third. Multifocal mild to moderate narrowing present post intervention in distal third. Focal occlusion in proximal DP could not be reached with Export aspiration catheter but recanalized with moderate to severe residual narrowing following the administration of NTG 200 mcg and tPa 2 mg. Very difficult to access PT and once wire access established, Viance crossing catheter and balloon catheter would not pass more than a few centimeters beyond the origin. Nitroglycerin 200 mcg administered at the level of the tibioperoneal trunk. Peri-anastomotic PT treated with 2 mm PTA. Remainder of PT not well seen, likely due to spasm. New occlusion in proximal third of peroneal also likely due to spasm as only guidewire passed transiently into peroneal but no intervention performed in or above this location. Tiny extravasation seen coming from a tiny collateral arising from the TP trunk, likely representing 014 wire injury. Likely not clinically significant. No intervention performed in this location.       Vascular sheath was removed and hemostasis achieved with Perclose.      Complications: No immediate complications.        

IMPRESSION:     Angiography of the right lower extremity demonstrates multilevel peripheral arterial disease.. The superficial femoral artery demonstrates diffuse luminal irregularity without significant stenosis, similar in appearance to post intervention images obtained on 7/31/2025. Popliteal artery is widely patent.
