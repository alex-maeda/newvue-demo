LOWER EXTREMITY ANGIOGRAPHY AND INTERVENTIONS
 
** HISTORY **:
85 years old, History of PVD status post right leg angiogram and intervention on 07/31/25. Presents for left leg angiogram.  
 
** TECHNIQUE **:
Procedural Personnel ***
Complications: No immediate complications.
 
PROCEDURE SUMMARY:
- Arterial access with ultrasound guidance
- Left lower extremity angiography as described below
- Arterial interventions as described below
 
PROCEDURE DETAILS:
 
Pre-procedure
Consent: Informed consent for the procedure including risks, benefits and alternatives was obtained and time-out was performed prior to the procedure.
Preparation: The site was prepared and draped using maximal sterile barrier technique including cutaneous antisepsis.
 
Anesthesia/sedation
Level of anesthesia/sedation: Moderate sedation (conscious sedation).  1 mg of IV Versed, 150 mcg of IV Fentanyl, 50 mg of IV Benadryl and 1 mg of IV Dilaudid was given.
 
Access
Local anesthesia was administered. The vessel was sonographically evaluated and judged to be patent. Real time ultrasound was used to visualize needle entry into the vessel and a permanent image was stored. A 5F sheath was placed.
Vessel accessed: Right common femoral artery
Access technique: 19 gauge access needle
 
Aortography
Indication for aortography: Diagnostic angiography - A prior study is available, but the patient's condition with respect to the clinical indication has changed since the prior study.
Vessel catheterized: Abdominal aorta below the level of the renal arteries
Findings: No significant iliac artery stenosis. Slow flow of blood, due to likely low ejection fraction.
 
Left lower extremity angiography and interventions
The left lower extremity arterial system was catheterized using Flush catheter followed by 5F long sheath.
Indication for angiography: Diagnostic angiography - There was no prior catheter-based angiographic study available and a full diagnostic study was performed. The decision to intervene was based on the diagnostic study.
 
Local lidocaine. Right CFA accessed with micropuncture set under ultrasound guidance and 5F sheath placed. Flush catheter with limited pelvic angiogram without significant stenosis. 5F long sheath placed in left SFA. Multifocal stenoses noted in left SFA secondary to atherosclerotic disease, successfully treated with 4 mm Shockwave E8 lithotripsy balloon (400 total pulses). Followed by 5 mm InPACT drug coated plasty of proximal/mid SFA, 4 mm drug coated plasty of mid/distal SFA and 3 mm plasty of popliteal/left proximal ATA. Final angiogram with improved flow and runoff, but overall patient has low cardiac output due to slow flow. Left proximal peroneal and left proximal/mid PTA are chronically occluded unable to cross on today's exam. All sheaths, catheter and wires removed. Hemostasis obtained with Mynx closure device. Skin glue applied, steri-strips placed and sterile dressing placed. 3000 units of IV heparin was given during case. Postprocedure ACT after IV heparin as given was 181.
