
Impression
Ultrasound-guided biopsy of 2 cm right supraclavicular lymph node, as described.
 
Plan: 
Specimen(s) sent for evaluation.
 
 Narrative
ULTRASOUND-GUIDED BIOPSY
 
** HISTORY **:
65 years old, history of lymphadenopathy with concern for metastatic disease. Presents for image guided right supraclavicular lymph node biopsy.   
 
 
** TECHNIQUE **:
Procedural Personnel
***
 
Complications: No immediate complications.
 
PROCEDURE SUMMARY:
- Percutaneous US-guided biopsy of 2 cm right supraclavicular lymph node/mass.
- Additional procedure(s): None
 
PROCEDURE DETAILS:
 
Pre-procedure
Consent: Informed consent for the procedure including risks, benefits and alternatives was obtained and time-out was performed prior to the procedure.
Preparation: The site was prepared and draped using maximal sterile barrier technique including cutaneous antisepsis.
 
Anesthesia/sedation
Level of anesthesia/sedation: Moderate sedation (conscious sedation)
 
Biopsy 
The patient was positioned supine. Initial ultrasound was performed.
 
Local anesthesia was administered. Under US guidance, the biopsy needle was advanced to the target and biopsy was performed.
Coaxial needle: 17 gauge
 
Core needle biopsy device: Bard Mission
Core needle size: 18 gauge
Number of core specimens: 5 core samples placed in formalin and sent to pathology. 1 core sample in saline/microbiology. 
 
Needle removal
The biopsy needle was removed and a sterile band-aid was applied.
Tract embolization: None
 
** FINDINGS **:
There is redemonstration of abnormal appearing 2 cm right supraclavicular lymph node/mass, which is targeted for biopsy.  Patient's C-collar was removed just for procedure and placed back on after procedure. No complications or significant hematoma postprocedure ultrasound.
