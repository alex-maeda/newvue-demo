    DRAINAGE CATHETER PLACEMENT
** HISTORY **:    69 years old, female with diverticulitis and splenic abscess. She presented for splenic abscess drain placement
** TECHNIQUE **:  Procedural Personnel    Attending physician(s): Melika Rezaee, MD       Complications: No immediate complications.     PROCEDURE SUMMARY:  - Visceral drainage catheter placement under ultrasound guidance  - Additional procedure(s): None   PROCEDURE DETAILS:   Pre-procedure  Consent: Informed consent for the procedure including risks, benefits and alternatives was obtained and time-out was performed prior to the procedure.  Preparation: The site was prepared and draped using maximal sterile barrier technique including cutaneous antisepsis.   Anesthesia/sedation  Level of anesthesia/sedation: Moderate sedation (conscious sedation)       Drainage catheter placement  The patient was positioned prone. Initial ultrasound of left upper quadrant demonstrated septated splenic abscess. Local anesthesia was administered. The fluid collection was accessed using an access needle followed by wire insertion and serial dilation and a drainage catheter was placed. Position of the drainage catheter within the fluid collection was confirmed.  - Drainage catheter placed: 8.5 French Multipurpose drainage catheter  - External catheter securement: Non-absorbable suture
** FINDINGS **:  Loculated splenic abscess.     

IMPRESSION:     Percutaneous placement of a 8.5 Fr  French drainage catheter into splenic abscess, yielding 120 mL of purulent bloody fluid.
