    LEFT CHEST TUBE PLACEMENT
** HISTORY **:  69 years old Female, with left loculated pleural effusion concerning for empyema
** TECHNIQUE **:     PROCEDURE SUMMARY:  - Percutaneous pleural drainage with insertion of indwelling catheter under ultrasound guidance  - Additional procedure(s): None     Sedation: Mild sedation was achieved with intravenous medication including fentanyl  50  mcg.
** FINDINGS **:   PROCEDURE DETAILS:   Informed consent for the procedure including risks, benefits and alternatives was obtained and time-out was performed prior to the procedure.  The site was prepared and draped using maximal sterile barrier technique including cutaneous antisepsis.   The patient was positioned seated with torso upright. Initial imaging was performed revealing 2 loculated non-communicating collections. Local anesthesia was administered. The left inferior pleural space was accessed using a 5 French Yueh sheath needle.  Guidewire was advanced into the pleural space, subcutaneous tissues were dilated, and a 10 French pigtail drainage catheter was placed with the pigtail formed at the level of the lower hemithorax. 40 cc of purulent bloody pleural fluid aspirated from inferior collection and sent for lab analysis. Chest tube placement attempted by unable to aspirate any additional fluid. Chest tube cut and removed. Post procedure imaging  revealed decreased fluid in the hemithorax       The left superior pleural space was accessed using a 5 French Yueh sheath needle.  Guidewire was advanced into the pleural space, subcutaneous tissues were dilated, and a 10 French pigtail drainage catheter was placed with the pigtail formed at the level of the upper hemithorax. 60 cc of serous straw colored pleural fluid aspirated from superior collection and sent for lab analysis. Successful 10 fr skater pigtail placed. Position of the drainage catheter within the pleural space was confirmed. Post procedure imaging  revealed no significant change.The drain was fixed to the skin with non-absorbable suture and dry, occlusive dressing was placed.  The chest tube was then attached to pleurivac device.     Complications: No immediate complications.      

IMPRESSION:     Left-sided 10 French chest tube placement.
