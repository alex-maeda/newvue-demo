CHEST, 2 VIEWS
 
** HISTORY **:
68-year-old man with fever
 
Technique:  Frontal and lateral chest, 2 views.
 
Comparison:  CT abdomen from the same date.
 
** FINDINGS **:
Lines/tubes:  None.
Cardiac, mediastinal, and hilar contours:  Cardiomediastinal
silhouette and pulmonary vascularity are within normal limits.
Calcified plaque involves the thoracic aorta.
Lungs:  Linear bibasilar opacities are most suggestive of
atelectasis although superimposed consolidation cannot be
excluded. No appreciable pleural effusion or pneumothorax
detected.
Bones:  Grossly unremarkable.
Soft tissues:  Unremarkable.
 
** IMPRESSION **:
Linear bibasilar opacities are most suggestive of atelectasis
although superimposed consolidation cannot be excluded.
 
Aortic atherosclerosis.
